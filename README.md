## Why People Are Searching "Virtual Bank Account Crypto" in the First Place

**PokePay vs Tevau.** Tevau charges around 1% on top-ups but zero on spending transactions, and supports daily limits up to $3,000,000 — dramatically higher than PokePay's ~$12,500. If you're a high-volume spender, Tevau's no-spending-fee model saves meaningful money over time. PokePay's advantage is faster onboarding and lower entry cost for the virtual card.

**PokePay vs KazePay.** KazePay also charges zero spending fees, supports a broader range of blockchain networks for top-ups, and offers a 2% rebate. Its physical card runs $100-$150. KazePay is the stronger choice if you top up from multiple chains and want spending rebates; PokePay is simpler and cheaper to start.

The honest summary: PokePay is a strong **entry-level** option. Once your spending volume grows or your fee sensitivity sharpens, alternatives with zero spending fees start to look more attractive.

## Limitations and Honest Caveats

PokePay is not a universal solution, and pretending otherwise doesn't help anyone make a good decision.

**Restricted jurisdictions.** The platform does not operate in a long list of countries, including the United States, Russia, China, Iraq, Iran, Cuba, Afghanistan, and many others. Check the restricted jurisdictions list before investing time in the sign-up process. If you're in a restricted region, none of the rest of this guide applies to you.

**Fees compound for high-volume users.** The 1% + 1% + 1% stack (top-up + transaction + FX) is manageable for occasional spending but becomes a real annual cost for frequent users. If you're spending $5,000+ per month through the card, you're paying $150+ in fees that zero-spending-fee alternatives would avoid.

**No cashback program.** PokePay does not offer a dedicated cashback program. If earning on your spending matters to you, alternatives like Ether.fi Cash (which offers cashback in wETH) or Bybit (up to 10% for eligible users) are worth considering.

**Customer support is mixed.** User reviews on Trustpilot sit around 3.7/5, and reports on support responsiveness vary. Some users describe smooth experiences; others mention delays in issue resolution. Set your expectations accordingly.

**Network transparency.** PokePay does not clearly document which blockchain networks (TRC20, ERC20, BEP20, etc.) are supported for top-ups. This has caused problems for users who sent assets via an unsupported network. Confirm with support before transferring.

**Not anonymous.** KYC is mandatory. If you're looking for a no-questions-asked payment method, this is not the platform for it. The regulatory framework that makes the card work with mainstream merchants also requires identity verification.
